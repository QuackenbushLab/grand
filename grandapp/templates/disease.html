    Enrichment libraries

    TFs regulate the expression of genes. Whereas several tools allow to test the enrichment of a list of genes in disease pathways, this tool allows testing the enrichemnt of a list of disrupted TF activites in two disease libraries, namely, GWAS traits and Human Phenotype ontology disease as described in Lambert et al, Cell, 2018.

    The GWAS library connects several traits to transcription factors through assigning hits to TFs if they fall near their coding regions. The second library is a list of TF genes extracted from the list of genes in Human Phenotype Ontology.

    Morevoer, the TF list is tested for significant enrichment in two tissue-specific libraries, namely, tissue-specific TF expression and tissue-specific TF targeting as computed in Sonawane et al, Cell reports, 2017.

    The input is a list of TFs in gene symbols as in the example. 1639 genes define TFs as per the list established in Lambert et al, Cell, 2018.
